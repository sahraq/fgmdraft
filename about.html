Statistics

Somalia has one of the highest rates of Female Genital Mutilation (FGM) in the world, with 98% of women and girls aged 15-49 having undergone the procedure. Most undergo FGM between ages 5 and 11. The most common type practiced is Type III (infibulation), which involves the most severe form of cutting.

The practice poses significant health risks, contributing to increased maternal and newborn mortality rates. Women who have undergone FGM are more likely to suffer complications during childbirth, including hemorrhaging and obstructed labor, leading to higher rates of maternal deaths. In fact, FGM increases the risk of childbirth complications by 50% and newborn deaths by up to 55%.

FGM is also linked to a range of chronic health issues, such as urinary infections, chronic pain, cysts, and infertility. Studies show that about 30% of women who have undergone FGM suffer from complications that require medical attention later in life.
